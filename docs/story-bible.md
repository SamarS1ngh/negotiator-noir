# THE NEGOTIATOR — Story Bible

A noir port city, late 1940s. The waterfront belongs to the **Marlowe empire** —
shipping, loan-sharking, every racket that moves through the docks. Debt is the
weapon. Everyone here owes someone, and every chain of debt runs up to Marlowe.

The player is at the bottom of that chain, clawing up by turning the empire's own
people against each other. Every character below is a wound you can press.

---

## YOU — the dead man's child

- **Who:** The only child of **Tomas Vidal**, a small freight-and-chandlery man
  who borrowed from Marlowe to keep his business breathing.
- **The wound:** Marlowe's people didn't break Tomas's legs — they broke his
  *name*. Spread word he'd informed, cheated his own customers. The business
  died in a week. Tomas hanged himself in his own warehouse. You were away when
  it happened; you came home to a padlock and a debt with your name on it now.
- **The want:** Not the money. You want to take the whole thing *from* Marlowe —
  make the man who ruins people with a signature feel the floor come out.
- **The angle (why anyone listens):** You have nothing left to take. No money, no
  name, no one. A man with nothing to lose is the one thing a careful empire
  can't price. And you've spent a year in the dark, learning exactly who's afraid.
- **The mirror:** Ricci is what you could become — a nobody who climbed by
  turning cruel. The game keeps asking if you'll cross the same line.

---

## SAL MORETTI — the bookkeeper who did the numbers

- **Who:** Ricci's bookkeeper. Middle-aged, careful, drinks alone, flinches at
  Marlowe's name.
- **The wound / the secret:** Sal keeps two ledgers — the real one, and the one
  that proves **Ricci skims Marlowe**. That book is his death warrant if anyone
  learns he showed it. He's had it memorized for a year and done nothing, because
  doing something is how men like him end up in the harbor.
- **The emotional tie to YOU:** Sal is the man who *cooked your father's books* —
  wrote, on Ricci's order, the numbers that "proved" Tomas was hiding money he
  never had. He carries your father up three flights of stairs in his head every
  night. Naming yourself is a bomb you can drop on him.
- **The want:** To survive. Under that — to atone, if someone gives him a way.
- **The lever:** guilt (over your father) or fear (of Marlowe's coming audit).
  Money misreads him; he's not for sale, he's drowning.
- **Ties:** hates/fears Ricci · guilt toward you · terrified of Marlowe.

---

## THE CREW — Enzo and the loaders

- **Who:** The dock crew that moves Ricci's cargo. Led loosely by **Big Enzo**, a
  loader with a bad back and a dead-serious stare. Loyalty here is thin and
  follows whoever's winning — or whoever's safer to stand near.
- **The wound:** Ricci skims their wages and calls it "protection." When a young
  loader, **Pip**, crushed his hand on one of Ricci's rush jobs, Ricci docked the
  lost day. They haven't forgotten.
- **The emotional tie to YOU:** Your father once put half the dock to work in a
  lean winter and never called the debt. Enzo remembers Tomas Vidal as the one
  boss who was decent. That's credit you didn't know you had.
- **The want:** To be left alone and keep their own safe.
- **The lever:** resentment + fear of Marlowe's housecleaning. But they don't
  scare, and they smell a buyout a mile off — money makes them tip Ricci.
- **Ties:** fear Ricci · quiet debt to your father · move as a pack.

---

## BIANCHI — the collector who got passed over

- **Who:** A rival collector, sharp-suited and hungry. A *peer*, not a mark.
- **The wound:** Bianchi *built* the east docks — then Marlowe handed the prime
  territory to Ricci and left Bianchi the scraps. He's been swallowing that for
  three years.
- **The want:** Ricci's territory. All of it.
- **The lever:** proof Ricci is weak or skimming = the opening he's been starving
  for. He won't move on your word or your threat — he moves on *evidence* and
  *self-interest*.
- **The danger:** Bianchi is the one piece that can take everything for himself
  and cut you out. Using him is grabbing a knife by the blade.
- **Ties:** envies/hates Ricci · respects nerve, despises begging · sees you as a
  useful tool until you're not.

---

## RICCI — the collector, the target, your mirror

- **Who:** Marlowe's collector for the west docks. Proud, theatrical, cruel in
  public. The man who collected from your father — and, on Marlowe's order, the
  one who spread the word that broke Tomas's name.
- **The wound:** Ricci clawed up from the same gutter you're in. Marlowe once
  humiliated him in front of the other collectors — made him beg — and Ricci has
  never stopped feeling small. He **skims Marlowe** out of that old rage as much
  as greed. Under the theater, he is terrified, every day, of the man above him.
- **The want:** To be untouchable. To never be the small man in the room again.
- **The mask:** He humiliates debtors *because he was humiliated*. He doesn't
  threaten — he makes men beg where people can watch.
- **The secret:** the skim. If Marlowe learns it, Ricci is dead. That's the blade
  you carry to the sit-down.
- **Ties:** fears Marlowe · trusted by Marlowe (for now) · hated by Sal · envied
  by Bianchi · doesn't yet know your face — or that you're Vidal's child.

---

## MARLOWE — the empire, the cold signature

- **Who:** The top. Never raises his voice, never throws a punch. Ruined your
  father with a signature and a rumor. Reads people the way Sal reads ledgers —
  as balances to settle.
- **The wound (the only crack):** He does not feel loss — his power is the
  *absence* of feeling. But he prizes control absolutely, and the one thing that
  moves him to rage is being *stolen from* by his own. A traitor inside the
  machine is the lever: turn Marlowe's need for order against Ricci's skim.
- **The want:** The empire intact and obedient. Order above all.
- **Ties:** the apex — feared by all, close to none. Locked until you climb.

---

## THE EMOTIONAL WEB (what ties to what)

- **You → Ricci:** he broke your father's name in person. Personal.
- **You ↔ your father:** guilt you weren't there; the debt with your name on it.
- **Sal → your father:** wrote the false numbers. Guilt is his lever.
- **Crew → your father:** he was decent to them once. Goodwill is your credit.
- **Ricci → Marlowe:** fear + old humiliation + the skim he steals in revenge.
- **Bianchi → Ricci:** envy of the territory that should've been his.
- **Crew → Ricci:** squeezed wages, Pip's hand. Resentment under the fear.
- **You ↔ Ricci (the theme):** two nobodies who clawed up. One turned cruel. The
  game asks, every mission, whether you will too.
